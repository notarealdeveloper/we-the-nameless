## Numbers 2:32

------------------------------------------------------------------------

³² These are the counts of the children of Israel by their fathers’
house, all the counts of the camps by their armies: six hundred three
thousand five hundred fifty.

[image]

---

Footnote 2:32. six hundred three thousand five hundred fifty. Add to this the
underage and elderly men, the women of all ages, and the Levites, and
the total number of Israelites must approach two million. It has been
calculated that by these numbers, marching eight across, when the first
Israelites reach Mount Sinai, half of them would still be in Egypt! The
extraordinary size of this population is a famous old problem in
traditional and critical biblical scholarship. The numbers appear far
too high; but they do not appear to be entirely invented either, because
what would be the motive for contriving them in all of this
tribe-by-tribe detail for the first four chapters of Numbers? Some
suggest that the word for “thousand” here means rather a “clan,” but
that is not correct (see the comment on Num 3:43). One possibility is
that these are the numbers from the census that is attributed to King
David (2 Samuel 24; see the comment on Exod 30:12). (There are just
three censuses in the Tanak, the two censuses of Moses and the census of
David.) Coming centuries later, in a period in which Israel is settled
in the land, the numbers are more understandable in the Davidic era
(though they are questionably high even for that period). In this
scenario, the records here would have come from old documents among the
archives which would have come to be mixed in with documents that were
used as sources for the Torah.
