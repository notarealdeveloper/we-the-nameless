because of contact with the dead body of a person.

## Leviticus 22:28

------------------------------------------------------------------------

²⁸ And an ox or a sheep: you shall not slaughter it and its child on one
day.

---

Footnote 22:28. it and its child on one day. It is possible that this is a
parallel to the commandment against cooking a kid in its mother’s milk.
This case is more commonly understood to be a moral matter—a respect for
the animals, accepting that although we eat animals we must appreciate
that they are living beings—whereas the case of the kid also has a
ritual dimension, reflected in a Ugaritic text. See comment on Exod

Footnote 23:19.
