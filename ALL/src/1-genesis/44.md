¹ And he commanded the one who was over his house, saying, “Fill the
men’s bags with as much food as they can carry and put each man’s silver
in the mouth of his bag,

---

Footnote 44:1. each man’s silver. The first time, when ten brothers come to
Egypt, Joseph imprisons Simeon, and he has their silver placed back in
their nine sacks. The second time, when they return with Benjamin,
Joseph releases Simeon, and he has their silver placed back in their
eleven sacks. The total number of portions of silver returned is twenty,
corresponding to the price that was paid for Joseph (37:28). It is yet
another case of a hidden link between acts of deception and their
payback in later events.
