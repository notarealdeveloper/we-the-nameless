## Genesis 39:14

------------------------------------------------------------------------

¹⁴ that she called to the people of her house and said to them, saying,
“See, he brought us a Hebrew man to fool with us. He came to me, to lie
with me, and I called in a loud voice.

39:14 to fool with us … to fool with me. She says “to fool with us” when
she tells the household, but she says “to fool with me” when she tells
her husband. Deceivers formulate their words carefully to persuade their
particular audience.

## Genesis 39:17

------------------------------------------------------------------------

¹⁷ And she spoke things like these to him, saying, “The Hebrew slave,
whom you brought us, came to me, to fool with me.

---

Footnote 39:17. whom you brought us. She uses the same device that Adam had used
when trying to exonerate himself in Eden when he said, “The woman, whom
you placed with me …” It is an attempt to help one’s position by putting
part of the blame on one’s judge. It works better for Potiphar’s wife,
using it on a human authority, than it did for Adam, who tried to use it
on God.
